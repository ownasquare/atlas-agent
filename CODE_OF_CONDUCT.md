# Code of conduct

## Our commitment

We want Atlas to be a welcoming project for people of every background and experience level.
Participants are expected to communicate with empathy, patience, and respect.

## Expected behavior

- Give specific, constructive feedback about the work rather than the person.
- Respect differing experience levels, identities, viewpoints, and communication styles.
- Accept correction, acknowledge mistakes, and help move discussions toward a useful outcome.
- Protect private information and follow the project's security-reporting process.
- Keep issues, reviews, and community spaces focused on the project.

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized attention, deliberate intimidation, personal attacks,
and publishing another person's private information are not acceptable.

## Scope

This policy applies in project repositories, reviews, issues, discussions, events, and public spaces
where someone is representing the project.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns through the project's
[private reporting form](https://github.com/ownasquare/atlas-agent/security/advisories/new) and begin
the report title with `Conduct report:`. Do not use a public issue when doing so could expose or
endanger someone. Maintainers may remove content, close discussions, restrict participation, or ban
a participant when needed to protect the community.

Reports will be handled as privately and fairly as practical. Retaliation against a person who raises
a good-faith concern is prohibited.
